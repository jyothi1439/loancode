Data Visualization

Data visualization is viewed by many disciplines as a modern equivalent of visual communication. It involves the creation and study of the visual representation of data. To communicate information clearly and efficiently, data visualization uses statistical graphics, plots, information graphics and, other tools. Numerical data may be encoded using dots, lines, or bars, to visually communicate a quantitative message.

* The above graphs describe how the loan will be approved for different attributes individually.

* For example in gender attribute, the male candidate has the highest rate of getting loan approval because of his work experience, Female has a low rate of getting loan approval. In Gender graph 0 represents female candidate and male candidates represent 1.

* The dependents attribute consists of 0,1,2,3 dependents in our dataset. when the dependents are less then there will be a high chance of getting a loan.

* In education, graduated persons have the highest chance of loan approval and ungraduate have the lowest chance of loan approval. From the above graph, 0 represents graduate and 1 represents ungraduate.

* In Married, Male has the higher chance of loan approved than the female. From the graph, 0 represents female and 1 represents the male.

* In the property area, if a person from urban or semi-urban will have the highest chance of loan approval than the person who is from rural. From the graph, 0 represents rural, 1 represents semi-urban and 2 represents urban.

* If self-employed, the person who has given self-employed as yes has the highest chance of loan approval than the person having a self-employed as no. From the graph,0 represents no, and 1 represents yes.
